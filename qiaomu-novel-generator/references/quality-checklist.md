# Quality Checklist

Use this checklist internally before returning a story. For samples or requested reviews, show the seven-item self-evaluation.

## Seven Checks

1. 开篇钩子
   - Does the first page contain danger, mystery, shame, desire, or irreversible loss?
   - Does the reader have a specific unanswered question?
   - Does the hook create curiosity about motive, identity, or danger without confusing the literal event?
   - If the opening uses an impossible or metaphorical phrase, is the literal situation anchored within the same sentence or next sentence?

2. 人物欲望
   - What does the protagonist want in visible, scene-level terms?
   - What private wound or fear makes the desire matter?

3. 爽点/情绪承诺
   - Is the main payoff clear: 爽, 甜, 虐, 恨, 惊, 燃, 怕, 痛, or治愈?
   - Did the story choose a high-pressure relationship, conflict arena, and 2-4 plot engines?

4. 经典重构
   - If famous works or writers were mentioned, were they reduced to reusable functions and craft sliders?
   - Did the new story change setting, relationship, stakes, object/rule, and ending enough to avoid a recognizable copy?
   - If a living author was named, did the prose avoid direct distinctive-style imitation?

5. 冲突升级
   - Does every major beat remove safety or increase cost?
   - Are there at least 3 escalations after the opening?

6. 对白张力
   - Do characters speak with hidden intent?
   - Can any explanatory line be replaced by pressure, action, silence, or implication?

7. 画面感
   - Are there concrete objects, physical actions, and sensory details?
   - Is the recurring image doing narrative work instead of decoration?

8. 反转/悬念
   - Does the story return earlier information with a changed meaning?
   - Is the reversal earned by clues, not dropped from nowhere?

9. 结尾余味
   - Does the ending resolve the present conflict?
   - Does the final image echo the opening and leave emotional resonance?

10. 去 AI 味
   - Does narration avoid `不是X，而是Y`, `不在于X，在于Y`, and slogan-like contrast?
   - Are transitions driven by events, images, and dialogue instead of `关键在于`, `值得注意的是`, or lesson voice?
   - Are decorative dashes and summary endings removed?

11. 开篇语义清晰
   - Does the opening avoid false genre signals such as undead, magic, dream, or absurdity unless those are intended?
   - Can a first-time reader explain the basic physical event without needing a later explanation?
   - Does poetic compression sharpen the scene rather than hide who did what?

## Repair Triggers

- If the story opens with history or worldbuilding, cut to the first wound.
- If the opening creates a false literal reading, replace the metaphor with a concrete condition or add an immediate physical anchor.
- If the protagonist only reacts, give them a risky choice.
- If the story lacks爽点, choose a clearer emotional payoff and add a visible pressure arena.
- If the plot feels generic, combine one relationship engine with one arena engine and one reversal engine.
- If the antagonist is generic, give them evidence, leverage, or a wound.
- If the climax is only physical action, add a moral price.
- If the ending explains the theme, delete the explanation and let an image carry it.
- If the prose uses formulaic contrast, rewrite the sentence as concrete action, image, or character pressure.
- If the first line is clever but unclear, keep the pressure and remove the riddle.

## Passing Standard

A sample passes when the story checks are at least "clear and functional", at least three are "strong", the opening does not create an unintended genre contract, and the anti-AI language check has no unresolved narration-level hits.

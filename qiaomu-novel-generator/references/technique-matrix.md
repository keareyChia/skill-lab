# Technique Matrix

Use this file when choosing the craft blend for a story. The goal is not to imitate an author. The goal is to combine reusable narrative functions.

Before choosing prose techniques, select the emotional payoff and plot engines from `references/story-engine-library.md`. The technique mix should serve those engines instead of floating as style.
If the user names a famous work or writer, consult `references/inspiration-remix-playbook.md` and translate the signal into craft sliders before drafting.

## Author Signal Translation

When the user names writers, genres, or famous works, translate the signal into craft terms:

| User signal | Use as general technique | Avoid |
|---|---|---|
| 大历史武侠、命运江湖 | moral debt, inherited pressure, sect/power politics, delayed revelation | copying sect names, martial arts names, character arcs, famous scenes |
| 极简江湖、冷对白、留白 | short pressure lines, silent threat, sudden reversal, image-first narration | exact sentence rhythm, signature phrasing, recognizable scene setup |
| 推理悬疑 | information gap, false answer, clue return, final reframe | copying cases, tricks, detective characters |
| 社会派现实 | ordinary person under institutional pressure, moral compromise, public consequence | real-person accusation without evidence |
| 浪漫爱情 | desire vs dignity, misread tenderness, costly confession, recurring object | melodrama without choice |
| 科幻概念 | one clean speculative rule, human cost, rule inversion, ethical dilemma | concept lecture, fake science overload |
| 奇幻志怪 | forbidden bargain, name/taboo, debt to the unseen, ritual detail | encyclopedia exposition |
| 都市爽感 | public humiliation, preparation, reversal, proof, moral price | effortless revenge or abuse fantasy |
| 短剧式强钩子 | compressed hook, escalation, cliffhanger, emotion-first scene | mechanical episode beats, fake cliffhangers |
| 网文期待感 | emotional payoff, visible growth, delayed release, next-level promise | endless憋屈 without partial payoff |
| 马伯庸式历史悬疑 | institutional detail, document clues, small person in a large system, light irony, dense reversals | direct imitation of living-author diction, copied plot/setting |
| 周星驰式小人物喜剧 | humiliation, absurd escalation, craft rediscovery, sudden sincerity, public comeback | copying jokes, lines, character setup, iconic scenes |

If the user asks for an exact style of a living author, say briefly that you will use the underlying craft features instead of direct imitation.

## Core Engines

Choose 3-5 engines. More than 5 usually dilutes the story.

### 1. Opening Hook Engines

- Dead object: a body, broken tool, unpaid letter, erased photo, impossible wound.
- Wrong identity: the protagonist is mistaken for someone powerful or guilty.
- Countdown: a debt, execution, wedding, memory deletion, train departure.
- Public shame: the protagonist is forced to act while others watch.
- Intimate impossibility: a loved one says something only the dead should know.

### 2. Desire Engines

- Survival desire: live through the next hour.
- Recognition desire: prove one truth no one believes.
- Love desire: protect someone who cannot know the sacrifice.
- Power desire: get one tool, name, secret, or seat.
- Freedom desire: escape a role, debt, prophecy, contract, or memory.

### 3. Conflict Escalation Engines

- The safe route closes.
- The helper is compromised.
- The apparent villain has evidence.
- The protagonist's strength creates a worse problem.
- A private secret becomes public.
- The clock moves earlier.
- The price becomes moral, not only physical.

### 4. Dialogue Tension Engines

- Each line should hide at least one of: threat, desire, accusation, test, bargain, grief.
- Let characters answer the pressure, not the literal question.
- Use silence or action beats when explanation would weaken the scene.
- Remove greetings, filler, and repeated information.

### 5. Image And Motif Engines

Pick one recurring object or image:

- rain, candle, old coin, scar, mirror, train ticket, paper crane, unpaid bill, cracked screen, red string, ash, snow, key.

Return to it at the ending with changed meaning.

### 6. Reversal Engines

- The gift was a warning.
- The witness caused the event.
- The enemy protected the protagonist from a worse enemy.
- The protagonist already paid the price before the story began.
- The lost memory was self-defense.
- The prophecy was written by the victim.

### 7. Ending Aftertaste Engines

- Pay off the opening image with a new meaning.
- Let the protagonist win the external conflict but lose innocence, safety, or certainty.
- Let the final line open a larger question without withholding the current story's resolution.
- Avoid explaining the theme after the emotional turn has landed.

## Use With Story Engines

Map engines to craft techniques:

- Hidden identity needs seeding, public arena, and witness reaction.
- Rebirth revenge needs parallel scene design: same trap, changed choice.
- Contract binding needs terms that change after every major scene.
- Progression ladder needs visible metrics plus emotional cost.
- Conspiracy clue chain needs clue return, false certainty, and earned reframe.
- Auction/appraisal needs concrete object detail, competing bidders, and value reversal.
- Outsider-ritual stories need local-rule unfamiliarity plus deeper domain proof.

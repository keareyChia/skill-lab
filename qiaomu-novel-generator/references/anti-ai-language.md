# Anti-AI Language Gate

Use this before returning story prose or a visible story plan.

The aim is not to make language plain. The aim is to remove formulaic AI signals so the story sounds written by a human with taste, pressure, and scene sense.

## Hard Smell: Not-X-But-Y

Avoid these patterns in narration:

- `不是X，而是Y`
- `不是X。不是Y。而是Z。`
- `问题不在于X，问题在于Y`
- `真正的X不是Y，而是Z`

Target: zero in story narration. Allow at most one only if it is character-specific dialogue and sounds like that person would actually say it.

Prefer concrete replacements:

| AI-ish pattern | Better move |
|---|---|
| `他不是害怕死亡，而是害怕被遗忘。` | `他怕死，也怕棺材合上以后，没人记得他来过。` |
| `这不是失败，而是一次机会。` | `失败两个字还贴在墙上，他已经把第二张图纸摊开。` |
| `真正的敌人不是对手，而是自己。` | `对手还没出刀，他先听见自己手腕在抖。` |
| `她不是想赢，而是想证明自己。` | `她想赢。更想看见那群人闭嘴。` |

## Other AI-Smell Patterns

Search and remove or rewrite:

- `总之`、`综上所述`、`总而言之`
- `关键在于`、`值得注意的是`、`有意思的是`
- `让我们`、`想象一个世界`
- `这不仅是...更是...`
- `这就是...的意义`
- repeated decorative `——`
- slogan-like summary after the ending

## Fiction-Specific Rewrites

- Replace explanation with action: do not say the hero is humiliated; show who laughs, who looks away, what object is taken.
- Replace theme with image: do not explain the ending; return to a concrete object with changed meaning.
- Replace abstract transition with event: instead of `危机真正开始了`, make someone knock, lie, bleed, vanish, bid, or betray.
- Replace lesson voice with character pressure: let dialogue threaten, bargain, accuse, test, or conceal.

## Hard Smell: Misleading Poetic Hook

Strong hooks can be compressed, strange, or poetic, but they must not accidentally change the reader's genre expectation.

Avoid opening lines that describe impossible action as literal fact unless the story is intentionally supernatural, horror, mythic, or absurdist.

Common failure:

| Misleading hook | Why it fails | Clearer move |
|---|---|---|
| `死人走进了酒肆。` | Readers may think a corpse or undead figure is walking. | `一个快死的人走进了酒肆。` |
| `尸体开口说话。` | Sounds like resurrection unless immediately anchored. | `那人离断气只剩一口，嘴唇还在动。` |
| `他死后第三天来赴约。` | Promises ghost logic. | `讣告贴出第三天，一个披着他旧衣的人来赴约。` |
| `一座城跪下了。` | Too abstract unless followed by a physical image. | `城门前跪满了人，连守将的甲叶都贴在雪泥里。` |

Safe rule:

- If the impossible phrase is literal in this world, keep it and make the genre promise clear.
- If it is metaphor, replace it with the literal condition, or add the anchor in the next sentence.
- In the first three paragraphs, do not make readers spend attention decoding basic physical facts. Save ambiguity for motive, identity, guilt, and danger.
- Prefer `快死的人`, `断气前的人`, `满身血的人`, `被误认为死人`, `披着死者衣物的人` over `死人` when the person is still alive.

## Self-Check

Before returning a story, scan for:

```text
不是.*而是
不在于.*在于
总之|综上所述|总而言之
关键在于|值得注意的是|让我们|想象一个世界
这不仅.*更是
这就是.*的意义
死人.*走|尸体.*走|死者.*开口|尸体.*开口
```

If any hit appears in story narration, rewrite it unless there is a deliberate character-voice reason.
For impossible-action hits in the opening, keep them only when the genre is intentionally supernatural or the next sentence gives a concrete literal anchor.
